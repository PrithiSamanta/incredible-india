Meghalaya: The Abode of Clouds

History & Culture

Meghalaya, meaning "Abode of Clouds" in Sanskrit, is a mesmerizing green plateau separated from the Assam valley by abrupt slopes. It is home to three major tribes—the Khasis, Garos, and Jaintias—who follow a unique matrilineal system where lineage and inheritance are traced through women, and the youngest daughter (*Khatduh*) inherits the ancestral property.

The state is a geological wonder, famous for its ancient limestone caves and the ingenious Living Root Bridges. These bridges, hand-woven from the roots of rubber trees (*Ficus elastica*) by the tribes over centuries, stand as a testament to the harmony between man and nature.

Interesting Facts

Some Interesting Facts about Meghalaya:

  • Wettest Place on Earth: Mawsynram holds the Guinness World Record for the highest average annual rainfall, beating its neighbor Cherrapunji.
  • Cleanest Village: Mawlynnong was accorded the title of the "Cleanest Village in Asia" in 2003.
  • Whistling Village: In the village of Kongthong, every resident has a unique tune (Jingrwai Iawbei) used as their name to call out to each other in the forest.

Must-Try Local Flavors - Cuisine

  • Jadoh: The signature dish of the Khasis—red rice cooked with pork and distinct spices.
  • Doh Khleh: A refreshing pork salad garnished with onions, chillies, and ginger.
  • Tungrymbai: A fermented soybean chutney that has a strong aroma and rich flavor, usually eaten with rice.

Destinations

Shillong

The Scotland of the East

  • Vibe: Colonial & Hip
  • Key Attraction: Umiam Lake (Barapani), a massive man-made lake perfect for kayaking, and the Don Bosco Museum.
  • Don't Miss: Shopping at Police Bazar and walking through the pine trees of Shillong Peak.

Cherrapunji (Sohra)

Land of Waterfalls

  • Vibe: Misty & Dramatic
  • Key Attraction: Nohkalikai Falls (India's tallest plunge waterfall) and the Seven Sisters Falls.
  • Don't Miss: Exploring the limestone formations inside Mawsmai Cave and Arwah Cave.

Dawki & Shnongpdeng

The Crystal River

  • Vibe: Transparent & Surreal
  • Key Attraction: The Umngot River, famous for water so clear that boats appear to be floating in mid-air.
  • Don't Miss: Camping by the riverside in Shnongpdeng and visiting the Indo-Bangladesh border at Tamabil.

Nongriat

The Root Bridges

  • Vibe: Adventurous & Raw
  • Key Attraction: The UNESCO-recognized Double Decker Living Root Bridge.
  • Don't Miss: The grueling but rewarding 3,500-step trek down to the village and a dip in the natural Rainbow Falls pool.

How to reach

✈️ By Air

  • Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi Airport (Guwahati): This is the most practical entry point. It is about 3-4 hours (120km) from Shillong.
  • Shillong Airport (Umroi): Located 30km from the city. It has limited connectivity (mostly from Kolkata) and flights are often cancelled due to weather.

🚆 By Rail

  • Guwahati Railway Station: The nearest major railhead. Meghalaya does not have a railway network inside the state yet.
  • Mendipathar Station: A small station in the Garo Hills, but not useful for tourists visiting Shillong/Cherrapunji.

🚗 By Road

The drive from Guwahati to Shillong is one of the best in India.

  • NH6 (GS Road): A 4-lane highway that winds through the hills. You can hire shared sumos or private taxis (Ola/Uber outstation) from Guwahati.
  • Bus: ASTC and private buses run frequently from Paltan Bazar (Guwahati) to Shillong.
  • Bike Rentals: Available in Guwahati and Shillong for those who want to ride through the clouds.

Itineraries

Choose Your Vibe

The Classic Cloud Circuit

Best for: Families, couples, and first-time visitors.

Day 1: The Ascent

Route: Guwahati ➔ Shillong

  • Activity: Stop at Umiam Lake for a boat ride. Check into your hotel in Shillong.
  • Stay: Shillong (Police Bazar or Laitumkhrah).

Day 2: City of Rock

Route: Shillong Local

  • Activity: Visit Don Bosco Museum, Ward’s Lake, and Shillong Peak. Cafe hopping in the evening (Dylan's Cafe).
  • Stay: Shillong.

Day 3: Wettest Place

Route: Shillong ➔ Cherrapunji (Sohra)

  • Activity: Drive to Sohra. Visit Nohkalikai Falls, Mawsmai Cave, and Seven Sisters Falls.
  • Stay: Cherrapunji (Resort overlooking the valley).

Day 4: Crystal Waters

Route: Sohra ➔ Dawki ➔ Mawlynnong

  • Activity: Drive to Dawki for a boat ride on Umngot River. Visit Mawlynnong (Cleanest Village) on the way back.
  • Stay: Mawlynnong (Homestay) or return to Shillong.

Day 5: The Grand Canyon

Route: Laitlum Canyons

  • Activity: Visit Laitlum Canyons for a breathtaking view of the gorge. Ideal for photography.
  • Stay: Shillong.

Day 6: Departure

  • Activity: Drive back to Guwahati. Stop for pineapples at Nongpoh.
  • Stay: Departure.

The Trekker's Paradise

Best for: Adventurers, hikers, and nature enthusiasts.

Day 1: Arrival

Route: Guwahati ➔ Shillong

  • Activity: Arrive in Shillong. Visit the Sacred Grove forests in Mawphlang.
  • Stay: Shillong.

Day 2: The David Scott Trail

Route: Mawphlang ➔ Lad Mawphlang

  • Activity: A 16km historic trek through trails used by the British. Moderate difficulty.
  • Stay: Cherrapunji.

Day 3: The 3500 Steps

Route: Sohra ➔ Nongriat

  • Activity: Trek down to Nongriat village to see the Double Decker Living Root Bridge. Swim in natural pools.
  • Stay: Nongriat (Basic Homestay).

Day 4: Rainbow Falls

Route: Nongriat Exploration

  • Activity: Hike further to Rainbow Falls (bright blue water). Trek back up to Sohra by evening.
  • Stay: Cherrapunji.

Day 5: Caving Adventure

Route: Maswsmai/Krem Mawmluh

  • Activity: Go caving with a guide in Krem Mawmluh or explore the Wei Sawdong 3-tier waterfall.
  • Stay: Cherrapunji.

Day 6: Riverside Camping

Route: Sohra ➔ Shnongpdeng

  • Activity: Drive to Shnongpdeng. Engage in Cliff Jumping, Ziplining, and overnight camping by the river.
  • Stay: Shnongpdeng (Tent).

Day 7: Departure

  • Activity: Drive straight to Guwahati Airport (5 hours).
  • Stay: Departure.

Best Time to Visit

Time Best For
October to April Winter/Spring: This is the only time the Dawki river is crystal clear. Weather is cool and pleasant.
June to September Monsoon: Best for witnessing the waterfalls in their full glory. However, it rains continuously, and views may be foggy.
November Cherry Blossom Festival: Shillong turns pink with blooming cherry blossoms.

Essential Travel Tips for Meghalaya

Important things to know before you pack your bags.

1. Shillong Traffic is Real

Shillong has narrow roads and heavy traffic jams, especially near Police Bazar. Walk whenever possible. If you have a flight to catch from Guwahati, leave Shillong at least 4-5 hours in advance.

2. The Clarity of Dawki

The famous photos of boats floating on invisible water are only possible in Winter (Oct-Mar). In the monsoon, the river turns muddy due to rain. Plan accordingly.

3. Pack for Rain

You are visiting the wettest place on earth. Even in the dry season, showers can happen. Always carry a poncho or umbrella and water-resistant shoes.

4. Cash is Necessary

While Shillong accepts cards/UPI, remote areas like Nongriat, Dawki, and small roadside dhabas run on cash. ATMs are scarce outside the capital.

5. Respect the Matrilineal Culture

Women hold a high status in Meghalayan society. It is one of the safest states for solo female travelers. Be respectful and polite to locals.

6. Sunday Shutdown

Meghalaya is predominantly Christian. On Sundays, almost everything (shops, taxis, markets) is closed or operates at very low capacity. Plan your Sundays for nature walks or relaxation, not shopping.
