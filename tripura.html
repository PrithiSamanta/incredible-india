Tripura: The Queen of Eastern Hills

History & Culture

Tripura is a land of deep historical roots, once a princely state ruled by the Manikya Dynasty for centuries before merging with India in 1949. It is a unique cultural melting pot where 19 indigenous tribal communities (like the Tripuris, Reangs, and Jamatias) live in harmony with a significant Bengali population.

The state is famous for its royal palaces that float on water, ancient rock-cut carvings that rival the best in India, and a landscape dotted with bamboo forests and rubber plantations. It is a quiet, intellectual, and artistic hub, deeply influenced by the Nobel Laureate Rabindranath Tagore, who had close ties with the Tripura royal family.

Interesting Facts

Some Interesting Facts about Tripura:

  • The Lost Hill of Faces: Unakoti is an archaeological mystery featuring massive rock-cut sculptures of Shiva and other deities. Legend says there are 99,99,999 images—one less than a crore (*Koti*).
  • Only Lake Palace in NE: Neermahal is the only water palace in Eastern India and one of only two in the entire country (the other being in Udaipur, Rajasthan).
  • Bamboo Architects: Tripura is one of the largest bamboo producers in India, crafting everything from furniture to intricate art pieces.

Must-Try Local Flavors - Cuisine

  • Mui Borok: The traditional Tripuri platter, which is oil-free and healthy, centering on the key ingredient—Berma.
  • Berma: Fermented dried fish that adds a distinct salty and spicy punch to curries and chutneys. It is an acquired taste but essential to the cuisine.
  • Bangui: A delicious cake made of sticky rice and pork (or plain), wrapped in a special leaf (Lairu) and boiled.

Destinations

Agartala

The Royal Capital

  • Vibe: Relaxed & Regal
  • Key Attraction: Ujjayanta Palace, a stunning white Indo-Saracenic structure that now houses the State Museum.
  • Don't Miss: The Flag Lowering Ceremony at the Akhaura Border (Indo-Bangladesh border), similar to Wagah but more intimate.

Unakoti

The Shiva Pilgrimage

  • Vibe: Ancient & Mystical
  • Key Attraction: The giant 30-foot head of Unakotiswara Kal Bhairava carved into the vertical rock face amidst deep forests.
  • Don't Miss: The Ashokastami Mela held here annually in April.

Neermahal

The Water Palace

  • Vibe: Romantic & Scenic
  • Key Attraction: A red-and-white palace built in the middle of Rudra Sagar Lake.
  • Don't Miss: A boat ride to the palace at sunset, watching migratory birds land on the lake.

Jampui Hills

The Land of Eternal Spring

  • Vibe: Misty & Cool
  • Key Attraction: The highest hill range in the state, famous for its Orange Orchards and distinct Mizo culture (Lushai tribes inhabit this area).
  • Don't Miss: The Orange Festival in November and the view of the sunset over the hills of Mizoram.

How to reach

✈️ By Air

  • Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport (Agartala): The second busiest airport in Northeast India.
  • Connectivity: Excellent daily flights from Kolkata, Delhi, Guwahati, and Bangalore.

🚆 By Rail

  • Agartala Railway Station: A beautiful station connected to the main Indian railway grid via the "Chicken's Neck" corridor and Assam.
  • Notable Trains: The Rajdhani Express and Tripura Sundari Express connect directly to Delhi.

🚗 By Road

Roads are generally good, connecting all major tourist spots.

  • NH 8: The lifeline connecting Tripura to Assam and the rest of India.
  • Transport: State buses (TRTC), small cars, and auto-rickshaws (Tom-Toms) are the most common way to get around cities.

Itineraries

Choose Your Vibe

The Royal & Religious Trail

Best for: History buffs, families, and pilgrims.

Day 1: Arrival

Route: Agartala City

  • Activity: Arrive in Agartala. Visit the Ujjayanta Palace (Museum) and the Heritage Park.
  • Stay: Agartala.

Day 2: Shakti Peeth

Route: Agartala ➔ Udaipur

  • Activity: Drive to Udaipur (Tripura's temple town). Visit Tripura Sundari Temple (Matabari), one of the 51 Shakti Peethas.
  • Stay: Udaipur or return to Agartala.

Day 3: The Lake Palace

Route: Udaipur ➔ Melaghar

  • Activity: Visit Neermahal (Melaghar). Take a boat ride to explore the palace.
  • Stay: Sagarmahal Tourist Lodge (Lake view).

Day 4: Border Ceremony

Route: Melaghar ➔ Agartala

  • Activity: Return to Agartala. Visit Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary (Clouded Leopards) en route. Watch the Border Ceremony at Akhaura in the evening.
  • Stay: Agartala.

Day 5: Departure

  • Activity: Shopping for bamboo crafts at Purbasha Emporium before flying out.
  • Stay: Departure.

The Hills & Carvings Trail

Best for: Nature lovers and photographers.

Day 1: Arrival

Route: Agartala

  • Activity: Land in Agartala. Enjoy local street food (Momos and Mughlai Parathas).
  • Stay: Agartala.

Day 2: Faces in Rock

Route: Agartala ➔ Unakoti

  • Activity: Drive to Kailashahar/Unakoti (4 hours). Explore the massive rock carvings and trek the hill stairs.
  • Stay: Unakoti Tourist Lodge.

Day 3: Up the Hills

Route: Unakoti ➔ Jampui Hills

  • Activity: Drive up to Vanghmun village in Jampui Hills. Experience the cool climate and orange orchards.
  • Stay: Jampui Hills (Eden Lodge).

Day 4: Sunrise Views

Route: Jampui Exploration

  • Activity: Watch the sunrise over the valleys. Walk through the Reang tribal villages.
  • Stay: Jampui Hills.

Day 5: Return Journey

Route: Jampui ➔ Agartala

  • Activity: Long drive back to the capital. Relax in the evening.
  • Stay: Agartala.

Day 6: Departure

  • Activity: Fly out from Agartala.
  • Stay: Departure.

Best Time to Visit

Time Best For
October to March Winter: The most pleasant time. Ideal for walking tours of Unakoti and boating at Neermahal.
October (Durga Puja) Festivals: Agartala celebrates Durga Puja with as much grandeur as Kolkata. A great cultural experience.
November Orange Festival: Held in Jampui Hills, celebrating the harvest of lush oranges.

Essential Travel Tips for Tripura

Important things to know before you pack your bags.

1. No Permit Needed

Unlike many other Northeast states, Indian citizens do not need an Inner Line Permit (ILP) to enter Tripura. You can travel freely.

2. Proximity to Bangladesh

Tripura is surrounded on three sides by Bangladesh. Your phone network might switch to an international roaming network near the border areas. Manually select your Indian carrier in settings to avoid charges.

3. Vegetarian Food

Tripura is a fish-loving state. While vegetarian food is available (thanks to the Bengali influence), options might be limited in tribal areas. Paneer and Dal are usually available in tourist lodges.

4. Bamboo Crafts

Do not leave without buying bamboo handicrafts. The workmanship is exquisite. The Purbasha Govt Emporium in Agartala is the best place for authentic items at fixed prices.

5. Dress Modestly

When visiting temples like Tripura Sundari or walking through villages, dress modestly. Comfortable walking shoes are a must for Unakoti as there are many stairs.

6. Language

Bengali and Kokborok are the main languages. Hindi is widely understood in Agartala and tourist centers. English works well in hotels and with officials.
